Ice hockey commentary

Once Liu Yapeng commentated on a game about the penguins. Due to the wonderful performance of the players he personally appreciated, Liu Yapeng himself commentated passionately. Later, he was praised by the penguins’ fans for being objective and neutral. He was happy, but his heart was beating, because penguins fans praised him for being objective and neutral, which probably meant that the commentary wasn’t. So he immediately went back and listened to the commentary twice, reviewing what might have rattled a fan’s nerves and taking notes to determine what not to say. At the end of last year, Liu Yapeng encountered a problem in the course of interpretation guru Yu Jia – what on earth are we explaining for the audience. Liu yapeng’s own answer is that we are explaining for the audience’s emotions, but also not only for the audience’s emotions. If only to explain the audience’s emotions, the commentator may fall into the situation of emotional outburst by hook or by hook, but deviate from objectivity and fairness. And this is liu Yapeng in the process of commentary hockey commentary this job a little bit of thinking.

The training base of Guangzhou’s local ice hockey club, Yangcheng Lightning Ice Sports Club, is here. Every training day, the arena echoes with a special symphony: there are shouts from young players, loud reminders from coaches, and cheering voices from parents of “ice parents” to their children.

Standing in the center of the field, Yu Xianghe is the teaching director of the team. This man from northeast China is a national athlete and a former member of the national ice hockey team. He has a lot of experience. The Yangcheng Lightning club was established in 2007 and now has five tiers: U6, U8, U10, U12 and U14. The students mainly come from Tianhe, Huadu and Baiyun districts, as well as from Foshan and Shenzhen.

On the other side of the field was a blond young coach from Ufa, Russia, named Roman Muratov, a former KHL player. “I’ve been here for two years.” “I’m happy here,” Roman said. Sid Abel: 100 Greatest NHL Players

The paperback book is old, its pulpy pages now a yellow-brownish color and its pasty binding worn. It has no publication date, but reading it reveals it’s from 1952. Although entitled “Detroit’s Big Three,” it’s not about the local automakers General Motors, Ford and Chrysler, but the trio of Red Wings pictured on its cover who made up one of the top lines in NHL history.

Author Ed Fitkin wrote of Sid Abel, the line’s center and the eldest of the three, “Abel will go down in Detroit’s club history as the greatest competitor and inspirational force the Red Wings ever had.”
Abel’s youthful wings Ted Lindsay and Gordie Howe – also terrific competitors and inspirations – would always agree. They eventually went on without him, each to an extraordinary career, but the veteran Abel helped mold them into Hockey Hall of Famers. He’d settle down the fiery Lindsay and motivate the laid-back Howe, while directing their movements and setting their tactics on the ice.

“I don’t know where I’d be without him,” said a young Howe. “He just has to whack me with his stick when I’m not playing well and say, ‘Get going,’ and that’s all I ever need.”

“Abel is the greatest of them all,” Lindsay said. “He seems to know more about what I’m going to do than I do myself and he’s always in the right spot.”

Sidney Crosby

Crosby has more than lived up to all the hype that preceded him before he was selected by the Pittsburgh Penguins with the No. 1 pick in the 2005 NHL Draft. He’s a generational talent regarded for much of the 2010s as the best player in the world. Just like Wayne Gretzky said in 2016, Crosby “has proven over and over that he’s the best player in the game today. And it seems like the more important the game, the more impact that he makes on a game.”

The native of Cole Harbour, Nova Scotia, played one season at Shattuck-St. Mary’s, a prestigious prep school in Minnesota, and two seasons with Rimouski of the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League. He led the QMJHL in scoring each time and helped Rimouski reach the Memorial Cup Final in 2005.

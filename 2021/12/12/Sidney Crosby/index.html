Sidney Crosby

Crosby has more than lived up to all the hype that preceded him before he was selected by the Pittsburgh Penguins with the No. 1 pick in the 2005 NHL Draft. He’s a generational talent regarded for much of the 2010s as the best player in the world. Just like Wayne Gretzky said in 2016, Crosby “has proven over and over that he’s the best player in the game today. And it seems like the more important the game, the more impact that he makes on a game.”

The native of Cole Harbour, Nova Scotia, played one season at Shattuck-St. Mary’s, a prestigious prep school in Minnesota, and two seasons with Rimouski of the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League. He led the QMJHL in scoring each time and helped Rimouski reach the Memorial Cup Final in 2005.
